# Supplementary Information
*Carried but Conditionally Used: A Frozen Held-Out Audit of Whether Chirality-Aware Molecular Fingerprints Predict Measured Same-Assay Enantioselectivity*

All supplementary tables and figures are generated directly from the hashed frozen artifacts by `gen_supp.py`, `make_figs.py`, and `null_regen.py`; no values are transcribed. Hashes are listed in §S12.

## S1. Cohort-filtering flow and target eligibility
Filtering funnel (Table S1, `STable_funnel.csv`): 827,880 tightened IC50 activities (IC50/=/nM/binding/confidence-9/single-protein, valid, non-duplicate) → 511,596 distinct compounds → **134,564 fully-defined chiral compounds** → 1,967 structural enantiomer pairs → **14 qualifying targets**. Target eligibility (frozen, data-only): ≥80 unique structural pairs; ≥40 Bemis–Murcko scaffold groups; ≥5 source documents; ≥50 scaffold groups for 5-fold support. No criterion referenced |Δy| or performance. Target nomenclature: figures and tables use compact gene-symbol labels; the four targets with common synonyms are ERK2 (MAPK1), ROR-γ (RORC), KCNH2 (hERG/Kv11.1), and PI3Kδ (PIK3CD). Immutable identities for all fourteen targets (ChEMBL target ID, official ChEMBL preferred name, gene symbol, UniProt accession, organism, target type) are tabulated in `target_metadata_14.csv`/`.json`. For clarity, KCNH2/hERG (tid 165, CHEMBL240, UniProt Q12809) is a *Homo sapiens* voltage-gated potassium channel (Kv11.1); it is not a Kir/KCNJ inwardly-rectifying channel — the "inwardly rectifying" wording in ChEMBL's preferred-name string is a database-label artifact, and the authoritative identity is fixed by the ChEMBL ID, gene, and UniProt accession. KCNH2/hERG carries slightly negative held-out predictive skill (S_Δ = −0.007) and is not a positive target.

## S2. Enantiomer-pair definition and validation
A retained pair has identical non-stereochemical connectivity and complete inversion of every genuine stereogenic centre (InChI /m-flag construction, /t retained → diastereomers and E/Z excluded; isotopes excluded), with both members fully-specified single stereoisomers. **Stratified manual audit of 200 pairs** (oversampling multi-stereocentre, macrocyclic, salt-stripped, and extreme/near-zero-Δy cases): **0 pairing errors, 95% Wilson CI [0.0%, 1.9%]**.

## S3. Four-stage label construction
(1) collapse repeated measurements of a compound within one assay to their median pChEMBL; (2) within each shared assay compute Δy = pChEMBL(A) − pChEMBL(B); (3) aggregate assay-level Δy for the same target–pair by median; (4) retain one Δy per target–pair. Result: 1,967 labels (`cohort_pairs.csv`).

## S4. Repeat-provenance and measurement variability
Provenance classes among 17,676 repeated (compound, assay) groups (Table S2, `STable_provenance.csv`): **72.2% non-identical but sharing a source record (not independent)**; **15.2% (2,688) provenance-distinct, non-identical repeated-measurement groups** (distinct records and values); 10.8% identical/same-record; 1.7% identical/distinct-record. From the provenance-distinct groups the robust global single-measurement SD was 0.318 log (median), giving **σ(Δy) ≈ 0.45 as an approximate secondary reference** (assumes independence and a paired-error correlation; secondary use only, never for filtering). Per-target replication was too sparse for stratified error models (median 2 provenance-distinct groups per target).

## S5. Controls
- **Antisymmetry:** the symmetrized estimator satisfies max |f(A,B)+f(B,A)| = 4×10⁻¹⁶ (exact).
- **Chirality-strip (collision floor):** the achiral fingerprint gives δ ≡ 0 for every enantiomer pair → Δ̂ ≡ 0 → S_Δ exactly equals the zero-Δ baseline.
- **Orientation:** per-pair orientation randomization (seed 7); real metrics invariant (antisymmetry + symmetric augmentation), nulls correctly centred.
- **Sign-flip null centring:** mean −0.041 (range −0.077…−0.017), expected for a normalized skill score under scrambled labels; uncorrelated with cluster imbalance (ρ=−0.07) and target size (ρ=−0.26).
- **Ties:** exact ties |Δy| ≤ 10⁻⁹ excluded from balanced directional accuracy only; retained in S_Δ and Spearman.

## S6. Empirical null-distribution panels (all 14 targets)
Figure S-nulls (`SFig_nulls.png`): per-target histogram of the scaffold-cluster sign-flip null S_Δ with the observed S_Δ marked. Regenerated by `null_regen.py` (300 reps, primary RF) as a faithful realization of the frozen null procedure; regenerated mean/upper-quantile validated against the stored 2,000-rep summary in `cohort_results.json` (see `null_arrays.json`).

## S7. Scaffold-bootstrap confidence intervals
Per-target S_Δ 95% CIs (Fig 3): scaffold clusters resampled with replacement (matched count), member pairs pooled, S_Δ recomputed on the fixed out-of-fold predictions, 2.5/97.5 percentiles over 2,000 resamples.

## S8. Interpolation vs extrapolation
Table/Fig S-split (`S_interp_vs_extrap.json`, `SFig_interp_extrap.png`): connectivity-grouped interpolation vs scaffold-grouped extrapolation, per-target real S_Δ. Interpolation was marginally easier (median gap **+0.017**); the positive targets remained positive under the harder scaffold-extrapolation split used for all primary inference.

## S9. Full fingerprint collision audit
Table S-collision (`STable_collision.csv`, `SFig_collision.png`): per-target collision rate at radius 2 and 3. **For all 14 targets the collision rate was identical across 1,024/2,048/4,096/8,192 bits, count fingerprints, and unhashed identifiers** (config-invariant = True), excluding finite hashing; collisions fell substantially from radius 2 to 3 (e.g., BACE1 17.8→5.1%, BTK 3.5→0%). Manual classification of BACE1's radius-2 collisions: 15/21 radius-limited (resolved at r3), 6/21 genuine encoding (~5%), 0 hashing.

## S10. Complete target-level results and ridge-vs-RF
Table S-full (`STable_target_full.csv`): per target — held-out flag, n, scaffolds, tie fraction, r3 collision, S_Δ (RF and ridge), Spearman, primary and secondary empirical p, q(14-family), q(11-held-out-family), R_t. Paired ridge−RF difference: median +0.005, 95% CI [−0.032,+0.019], Wilcoxon p=0.76 (Fig 4).

## S11. Signal-to-variability: frozen vs exploratory (clearly distinguished)
- **Frozen (protocol):** R_t = median|Δy|/0.45. Association with S_Δ: all-14 ρ=0.62 (p=0.018); **held-out ρ=0.42 (p=0.20, not significant)**. Reported in the main text as a suggestive, unconfirmed trend.
- **Exploratory (post hoc, not frozen):** a tail-aware statistic (upper-quartile|Δy|/0.45) correlates far more strongly — all-14 ρ=0.81; **held-out ρ=0.77**. This was chosen *after* observing the HDAC6 counterexample and is **not confirmatory evidence**; it is a concrete, preregisterable hypothesis for future work (Table S-Rt `STable_Rt.csv`, `SFig_Rt.png`).
